Research

Overview

My research focuses on orographic precipitation and extreme rainfall associated with tropical cyclones in Taiwan and Japan. I combine polarimetric radar observations, rain gauges, and disdrometer observations to understand precipitation processes in mountainous regions. I am generally interested in the following topics:

  • Orographic enhancement of rainfall in tropical cyclone environments
  • Polarimetric radar analysis of precipitation microphysics
  • Dual-Doppler wind retrieval and storm kinematics
  • Hydrometeor classification and quantitative precipitation estimation (QPE) evaluation
  • Polarimetric radar operator for comparison between observations and numerical models

Recent Project

Typhoon Chanthu (2021) Case Study: Analyzed orographic precipitation using polarimetric radar data near eastern Taiwan. This work investigates how background precipitation modulates terrain-induced enhancement, with implications for forecasting localized extremes.
